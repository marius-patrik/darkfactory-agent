## Interpretation

**Is the 1M-from-RAM voice viable, and at what context sizes?**

The data gives a clear, honest answer: **yes at 4k, marginal at 32k, not viable above 32k under the D-026 "tune-to-feel" bar.**

The conv-7b-1m model delivers a 43.5-second TTFT at 4k tokens. That figure likely includes one-time model-loading overhead (the model was cold at test start; subsequent calls within a session would be faster). The 9.2 tok/s decode rate is borderline acceptable for a low-latency voice interface but is far below GPU-resident rates. At 32k tokens the TTFT balloons to 12.7 minutes — entirely because all context processing happens in RAM. The llama.cpp prompt-eval rate drops from 64.8 tok/s at 4k to 27.1 tok/s at 32k, reflecting the non-linear memory bandwidth cost of KV-cache growth in a RAM-swapped context. The 128k measurement exceeded the 25-minute time-box without yielding a first token.

For conv-14b-1m the picture is worse: even at 4k context the TTFT is 2.1 minutes, and at 32k the prompt-eval rate (22 tok/s) implies 24+ minutes for prompt processing alone, which the time-box correctly records as "exceeded 25m."

**D-026 "tune-to-feel" viability verdict:**

- **4k context / conv-7b-1m:** Barely usable for non-interactive batch tasks; 43-second cold TTFT is not voice-viable. Warm (cached) sessions may be faster but were not measured here.
- **32k context / conv-7b-1m:** 12.7-minute TTFT is unacceptable for any interactive use. Viable only as an offline document-processing endpoint where latency is unconstrained.
- **128k and above, both models:** Definitively not viable within the 25-minute time-box. The RAM-based architecture requires GPU offload or a smaller context window to meet any interactive bar.
- **conv-14b-1m at all context sizes:** Too slow for interactive voice. The 14b model would require VRAM offload of at least the attention layers to reach sub-minute TTFT at practical context sizes.

**Bottom line (cold path):** Cold full-context prompt-eval is minutes-slow at ≥32k — that is the physics of CPU prompt processing, correctly measured above.

## Warm-path addendum (measured after the cold runs)

The cold numbers above measure a **fresh slot evaluating the entire context** — the worst case. The standing brain's actual usage pattern is a **persistent session slot**: each turn appends tokens to an existing KV prefix, and llama.cpp's slot prompt-cache re-evaluates only the new suffix. Probe (conv-7b-1m, ~8k-token haystack, three sequential turns through the live gateway, `~/rommie-work/warm_ttft.py` on s002):

| Turn | Prefix state | TTFT | Total | Answer |
|------|--------------|------|-------|--------|
| 1 — cold | none | 99.9s | 100.6s | needle FOUND (`MARMALADE`) |
| 2 — warm, same prefix, new question | cached | **0.5s** | 0.9s | correct |
| 3 — warm again | cached | **0.5s** | 1.2s | correct |

**Corrected verdict:** the 1M-from-RAM voice is **viable in its designed usage pattern** — a standing conversation/thought worker holding a persistent slot pays prompt-eval only on each turn's new tokens (sub-second TTFT), with decode at ~9 tok/s (7B Q8). What remains slow is the **cold path**: session rehydration after restart/compaction, and bulk-context injection (pasting ~100k tokens mid-session ≈ an hour at 27 tok/s prompt-eval — that operation class must go GPU or cloud-1M). Engineering consequences for VS4:
- **conv-7b-1m over conv-14b-1m** as the voice default (9.2 vs 4.3 tok/s decode; 14B is too slow even warm).
- **Slot persistence is a first-class design item** (llama.cpp `/slots` save/restore + `--cache-reuse`) so restarts don't pay full re-eval; pair with the §05 context-cascade so the standing prefix stays append-only.
- **Cloud-1M (per §12.3) or GPU stays the escape hatch** for bulk-context surgery, not for the standing voice.
